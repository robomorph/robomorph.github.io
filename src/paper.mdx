## Abstract

Designing robot morphologies is time-consuming, dependent on human intuition, and ill-suited to exploring vast design spaces. We present **RoboMorph**, a framework that integrates LLM-based generation, evolutionary algorithms, robot grammars, and reinforcement learning to automate robot morphology design. Our approach uses best-shot prompting with GPT-4o as a mutation operator within an evolutionary loop, discovering terrain-specialized morphologies — wheeled quadrupeds, hexapods, and compact crawlers — that match or exceed designs from Robogrammar's graph search across four terrain types. Notably, these diverse morphologies emerge without hand-crafted mutation operators, explicit diversity objectives, or domain-specific heuristics.

</HighlightedSection>

## Framework

<Wide>
<Figure>
  <Picture slot="figure" src={robomorph} alt="Overview of the RoboMorph framework showing the six-step evolutionary loop" />
  <Fragment slot="caption">Overview of the RoboMorph framework: (1) prompt construction with few-shot examples, (2) LLM generates a new design with reasoning, (3) compile to XML, (4) RL policy training in MuJoCo, (5) fitness evaluation, (6) evolutionary selection updates the population.</Fragment>
</Figure>
</Wide>

RoboMorph operates as an iterative evolutionary loop. At each generation, the LLM receives a structured prompt containing the best-performing designs from the current population. It proposes a mutation — modifying limb topology, joint types, or body proportions — and outputs a new robot description in a domain-specific grammar. The candidate is compiled into a MuJoCo XML model, trained with reinforcement learning, and evaluated on terrain-specific locomotion. The resulting fitness score feeds back into the population for the next round of selection and mutation.

## Evolved Morphologies

Each terrain yields a distinct, specialized morphology — evidence that the LLM leverages implicit biomechanical knowledge to propose functional body plans.

<Wide>
<Figure>
  <Picture slot="figure" src={topDesigns} alt="Top robot designs evolved by RoboMorph for four different terrains" />
  <Fragment slot="caption">Top robot designs from RoboMorph per terrain: ridged (top-left), flat (top-right), frozen (bottom-left), and beams (bottom-right). Each terrain yields a distinct morphology, including wheeled and hexapod designs not found by prior methods.</Fragment>
</Figure>
</Wide>

**Ridged terrain.** A quadruped with elongated limbs and upward-swinging knee joints, enabling high foot clearance to step over periodic ridges.

**Flat terrain.** A wheeled quadruped with free-spinning wheels — a novel design not discovered by prior methods — that exploits rolling contact for fast, energy-efficient locomotion.

**Frozen terrain.** A hexapod with rigid, splayed legs creating a wide support polygon and low center of gravity, providing stability on slippery surfaces.

**Beams terrain.** A low-profile compact crawler with short limbs, morphologically similar to the flat design but adapted to clear overhead constraints.

## Comparison with Robogrammar

We compare RoboMorph's best designs against Robogrammar under both RL and MPC control. RoboMorph matches or exceeds Robogrammar across all terrains.

<TwoColumns>
  <Figure slot="left">
    <Picture slot="figure" src={rl} alt="Bar chart comparing RoboMorph and Robogrammar fitness under RL control" />
    <Fragment slot="caption">Evaluation using RL control across 8 seeds per terrain.</Fragment>
  </Figure>
  <Figure slot="right">
    <Picture slot="figure" src={mpc} alt="Bar chart comparing RoboMorph and Robogrammar fitness under MPC control" />

On flat terrain, RoboMorph's wheeled quadruped significantly outperforms Robogrammar's legged design. On frozen terrain, the hexapod's wide stance provides more efficient traction than Robogrammar's bipedal structure.
